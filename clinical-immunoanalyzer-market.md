<p><p>The clinical immunoanalyzer market consists of several types, including CLIA (Chemiluminescent Immunoassay), ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay), RIA (Radioimmunoassay), and FIA (Fluorescent Immunoassay). CLIA utilizes chemiluminescent detection for high sensitivity, while ELISA is widely used for its practicality and versatility. RIA, though less common now, employs radioisotopes for quantification. FIA uses fluorescent labels for detection, combining sensitivity with speed. Other market types may include various emerging methods and hybrid technologies that enhance diagnostic accuracy and throughput.</p></p>
