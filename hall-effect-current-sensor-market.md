<p><p>The Hall-Effect current sensor market is divided into two primary types: linear (analog) and threshold (digital) electroflu detectors. Linear sensors provide a continuous output proportional to the current flowing through a conductor, enabling precise measurements for various applications. In contrast, threshold sensors detect current levels against predefined limits, generating a digital output that indicates whether the current exceeds or falls below these thresholds. Both types serve critical roles in industrial, automotive, and consumer electronics, enhancing safety and efficiency.</p></p>
