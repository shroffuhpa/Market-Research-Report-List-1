<p><p>Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat narrowing or blockages in the coronary arteries, primarily due to atherosclerosis. This technique involves the insertion of a catheter through the skin into a blood vessel, guiding it to the coronary arteries. A balloon is inflated at the site of the blockage, and in many cases, a stent is placed to keep the artery open, improving blood flow to the heart.</p><p>The PCI market is poised for significant growth, driven by rising incidences of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in technology such as drug-eluting stents, and increasing awareness about early diagnosis and treatment options. Additionally, the aging population and lifestyle changes contributing to heart ailments further fuel demand for PCI procedures. The Percutaneous Coronary Intervention Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.00% during the forecast period. </p><p>Emerging trends include the development of bioresorbable stents, the integration of advanced imaging techniques to enhance procedural outcomes, and the growing emphasis on hybrid procedures. Overall, innovations in devices and techniques continue to shape the landscape of the PCI market, enhancing the quality of care in cardiology.</p></p>

<p><p>The Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI) market includes several key types of devices essential for treating coronary artery disease. Coronary stents are used to keep arteries open after balloon angioplasty. PTCA catheters serve to deliver balloons for dilating narrowed arteries. Coronary guidewires assist in navigating blood vessels during procedures. Embolic protection devices are designed to capture and remove debris during interventions, minimizing complications. Together, these devices enhance the safety and efficacy of PCI procedures, improving patient outcomes in cardiac care.</p></p>
