<p><p>Positron Emission Tomography (PET) systems are classified into three market types: Direct Imaging, Indirect Imaging, and Alternative Imaging. Direct Imaging involves using scintillation detectors that provide high-resolution images directly from emitted positrons. Indirect Imaging utilizes gamma cameras or other techniques to enhance imaging quality, offering a wider field of view. Alternative Imaging may incorporate advanced technologies, such as hybrid systems combining PET with MRI or CT, to provide comprehensive diagnostic information, enhancing clinical utility and patient outcomes.</p></p>
