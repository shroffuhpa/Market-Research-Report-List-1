<p><ul><li>Brakeshoe Brake System</li><li>Disc Brake System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The train brake system market comprises primarily two types: the brakeshoe brake system and the disc brake system. The brakeshoe system uses friction between shoes and wheels to slow down trains, making it a traditional choice due to its reliability. Conversely, the disc brake system utilizes a disc and caliper mechanism for braking, offering better heat dissipation and enhanced performance, especially at high speeds. Both systems are critical for ensuring safety and efficiency in rail transportation.</p></p>
